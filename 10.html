Immigrant

Carlos

Aspiring Partner for Finnish - Swiss girl
Mar 10, 2025

"Being in a foreign country means walking a tightrope above the ground without the net afforded a person by the country where he has family, colleagues, and friends, and where he can easily say what he has to say in a language he has known from childhood"

Milan Kundera, The Unbearable Lightness of Being

This post is about the adventure of being an immigrant. With its high moments and not so high.

This quote from Milan Kundera, every time that I read it, it makes me sigh. Of course there are difficult moments, the fact that you feel sometimes alone and misunderstood, not only the language but also the culture, the conexion, the jokes...

In Mexico like in every other country, you grow up with certain activities that become part of the culture. For example, in Mexico when I was kid there was a TV Program named "In Family with Chabelo" ("En Familia con Chabelo"), every sunday from 7 am to 10 am, it was about different games played by young and older people, and everyone woke up on sunday, turned on the TV and there he was "Chabelo"...

Chabelo was an actor, comedian, who used to speak with a childrens voice, as if he where a big kid...
From this character a lot of jokes were made of, like Chabelo was so inmortal that he survived the meteorite of the dinosaurs... anyway, silly and simple jokes but that always come in a conversation among mexicans, one have a laugh, is kind of nostalgic.

What does this has to be with being immigrant? Well, this small part of me only exists in Mexico, sometimes is kind of hard to express this part of me.

Obviously, on the other side, is the family. Of course we miss each other, that is why when we meet, we try to seize the moment that we have together, we got use to that.

Food I don't miss it too much, I can cook some mexican dishes and I hope I can make it for you someday.

There are some moments when one feel a little bit lost, sometimes in Mexico I don't feel like at home, there are some moments in Europe that I don't feel either. But I think is normal.
There is an old mexican movie from the 80-90s called "Neither from here, neither from there" and its about kind of the same topic, but from someone who goes to USA.

But not everything is tragic or dramatic. I like the adventure of being an immigrant and I enjoy it, getting to know another culture, another language... The opportunities that I have here in matters of job, quality of life, the chance to travel and meet people with similar mindset as mine... I don't change that for anything.

Most of my best friends are living the same that I am living, we all are immigrants and we don't have regrets.
If I didn't take the decision of getting out of Mexico, of course my life would be a lot different as now, I would have stayed in my comfort zone and I wouldn't have grown as a person the way I have done it.

All my decisions have brought me to this point. I am sitting in a bench in front of the Lake in Luzern, with a beautiful view in a sunny day. Putting my heart and my mind on paper for a woman that I really like and for some destiny reasons she is getting slowly into my heart. And I hope she stays there for a long, long time...

Un gran beso a la distancia
